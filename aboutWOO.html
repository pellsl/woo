Who is W.O.O.?

Henry Bryant Bigelow was a pioneering researcher for W.O.O. in the late 1890's. Here he is shown preparing for a dive to look at pretty fish.

World Ocean Observers is the world's premier oceanic research group. Having been established in 1889, W.O.O. was at the forefront of deep water research. They were the first organization to locate the up-to-then mythical whale shark in 1901, and have categorized and tagged at least 150 varieties of jelly fish.

Their research has been used by a wide variety of organizations to enhance the world's understanding of sea creatures, their environs, and the impact of humanity on them. Today, more than ever, W.O.O. is needed as we tackle climate change globally. They have had a profound impact on the Pisaster disaster that has been occurring for the last several years. They have been substantial contributors to research by The Atlantic National Geothermal Society, Hydrological Oceanographic Organization, and Young Engineers of Aquatic Health. They have also worked tirelessly for lesser known marine biota such as, the Sea Carrot, the North Pacific Gyre Isopod, and the Eastern Bulgogi Whale.
